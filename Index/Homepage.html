Find a perfect job for you

Website overview (motivation)

Many people have acquired a variety of skills in university. It is necessary for them to find out which skill can be used to find the best-fit job with a decent salary. Our website will help job seekers find the best job using different visualizations. We offer two main functions on our website including view job postings by salary and view jobs by skills.

Data

Our dataset was manually collected from open Ottawa job posting website. We have six attributes including job title, salary, date, skill, job category (part-time or full time) and job type (major).
Attribute types:
Quantitative: salary, date
Ordinal: skills
Categorical: job category (part-time or full time), job type (major)

Final Visualization

   Node-linked Diagram

      The structure includes three levels, with the skill that the user clicked at the center, the second level for all the work that needs this skill, and the third level for all the other skills that the job requires.

   Bobble Chart

      View jobs posting by salary: Different filters on the top menu including job type and salary range. When the user clicks on the filter, the job list and bubble chart will be updated. Linked highlighting function highlighting the selected job in the job list.

Conclusion

As for now, the main structure of our website and all the interactive visualizations are completed:

1. A homepage that contains all kinds of information about our website(an overview, data, tasks and sketches, etc.) as well as the two buttons which link to the two webpages.
2. A node-link diagram page, which includes three levels, with the skill that the user clicked at the center, the second level for all the work that needs this skill, and the third level for all the other skills that the job requires.
3. A bubble chart page, which users could view jobs by filter job category(full-time/part-time). After that, they could compare salaries with different job required skills.
In conclusion, we have finished 90% coding of our website with interactive visualizations. The next thing we'll do is finding problems and bugs by testing the website.
